Competitive landscape

Below is a series of likely competitors to the Spread app. Each of them perform different combinations of to-do lists, calendar events, password management, time tracking, and organization. Most of these are either quite simple, such as simply containing a list of tasks, or more specialized.

Evernote

Evernote provides one workspace to make notes, organize them, and systematically present ideas. It provides cross-platform note-taking services which makes it incredibly easy to create and manage notes across devices. It allows the user to upload images, audio, and videos besides taking notes.

Any.do

Any.do allows users to create reminders, events, to-do lists, notes, and more. It is a cross-platform app that will help you manage your daily schedule effectively. It is an award-winning app for its intuitive user interface and innovative features. It only takes a couple of minutes to organize one's day with this simple app. It renders seamless sync across devices and an extension is available for Chrome users. You can manage your tasks with your Chrome browser.

Due

Due is specifically designed for iPhones and iPads and allows you to jot down tasks and set reminders quickly. Using an exclusive app to track tasks is a good way to de-clutter a user's calendar. It is extremely easy to modify reminders with Due. If there is a change of plans, you can reschedule your plan by editing the reminder on the fly. This one-tap option to manage events makes it a simple app for managing events. The app also provides persistent reminders with auto snooze options. Until you mark the task as complete or reschedule the task, the auto snooze repeatedly notifies you of a task. Another excellent feature that sets it apart from other apps is the ability to send reminders to friends and colleagues.

RescueTime

If you have doubts if you are using your time sensibly, this app will send you weekly reports to indicate what things are stealing your time. Aside from its automatic time-tracking feature, this app also has distraction blocking and efficient report tools. RescueTime helps you avoid being overwhelmed by too many tasks. This app also has team software that’s dedicated to time management among team members. With this app, you may be shocked to discover how much time you’re wasting.

Clockify

Clockify is a time tracking software used by over two million people. It’s a popular time tracker and timesheet app that lets you track work hours across projects. Clockify is available for an unlimited number of users, completely free. It works in your browser, but you can also install it on your phone or computer for convenience.

LastPass

LastPass gives you a secure way to create, manage, and store your passwords so you never have to hit “Forgot password?” again. With the LastPass browser extension, a user can automatically fill in passwords when they are saved to their vault.

Trello

Trello is a flexible project management web application that lets users easily sort through their tasks and collaborate with their teammates. Instead of getting lost in a mess of emails or a confusing spreadsheet, Trello visualizes projects as lists pinned to digital bulletin boards. Each list is made of individual posts called cards, which can represent anything from tasks to ideas to goals. Team members can add comments, attachments, checklists, and custom labels to cards, keeping everyone on the same page.

Technical requirements

For Spread to function, the system it is running on must have the ability to receive inputs from a mouse or touch screen and receive a good amount of text so people can enter in tasks and information. The app will function best with an Internet connection, most likely with WiFi on mobile devices, alongside cloud syncing abilities, allowing it to sync between devices whenever possible. The system should load pages in under 3 seconds and store them locally in case the cloud connection is lost. The system should be able to allow the app access to other pieces of information on the device, such as related emails and messages. The user should be able to disable things like cloud service and disk access without degradation in any other app functionality.
