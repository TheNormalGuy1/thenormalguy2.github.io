Abstract design statement

The Spread app is a personal information and calendar task manager that assists the user in keeping track of life scheduling and serves as a storage location for information the user needs. There is a large load of information people need to keep track of throughout life, and a lot of that is attached to a date, such as appointment details and warranties. There are plenty of ways to store such information, but it is hard to do it in one place while having it accessible regardless of location or device. There are many options for password managers as well as calendars, but not as many combine the need for storage and scheduling.

Serving as something of a life manager, Spread takes in clues about things a user needs to store and remember, associating tasks and timeframes with each item as needed. There are many items that people need to be aware of on a repeating basis, including password resets, warranty expirations, subscriptions, OS security updates, and financial due dates. Alongside their everyday events, like classes or appointments, Spread fits these items into a user’s schedule and can bring them up at times when the user can effectively handle them.

People often find themselves needing to receive passcodes, part numbers, registration IDs, financial information, and license details. Users can dump all this into Spread and allow it to organize the data, splitting it into tasks, appointments/due dates, or both. However, having to enter such information manually is regularly necessary but also tedious. Spread can automatically parse notifications from your email and messages to break them down, offering to add the details to the app. For example, if a user gets an email saying that they have a package ready for pickup, Spark could add the pickup location into a task and produce a calendar notification for an appropriate time.

Setting audience and persona

Spread is targeted at technologically acute adults ages 20 to 40 of all genders who strive to maintain an independent lifestyle. They could be in school and/or work, where they may struggle to keep track of an abundance of information from their personal as well as professional lives. While they are independent, they likely have several others who depend on them to get things done as well as people whom they receive ongoing support from. They enjoy but also require efficiency, being able to perform tasks in the least amount of steps possible. Given their environment, they may need to regularly share information quickly with others.

Competitive landscape

Below is a series of likely competitors to the Spread app. Each of them perform different combinations of to-do lists, calendar events, password management, time tracking, and organization. Most of these are either quite simple, such as simply containing a list of tasks, or more specialized.

Evernote

Evernote provides one workspace to make notes, organize them, and systematically present ideas. It provides cross-platform note-taking services which makes it incredibly easy to create and manage notes across devices. It allows the user to upload images, audio, and videos besides taking notes.

Any.do

Any.do allows users to create reminders, events, to-do lists, notes, and more. It is a cross-platform app that will help you manage your daily schedule effectively. It is an award-winning app for its intuitive user interface and innovative features. It only takes a couple of minutes to organize one's day with this simple app. It renders seamless sync across devices and an extension is available for Chrome users. You can manage your tasks with your Chrome browser.

Due

Due is specifically designed for iPhones and iPads and allows you to jot down tasks and set reminders quickly. Using an exclusive app to track tasks is a good way to de-clutter a user's calendar. It is extremely easy to modify reminders with Due. If there is a change of plans, you can reschedule your plan by editing the reminder on the fly. This one-tap option to manage events makes it a simple app for managing events. The app also provides persistent reminders with auto snooze options. Until you mark the task as complete or reschedule the task, the auto snooze repeatedly notifies you of a task. Another excellent feature that sets it apart from other apps is the ability to send reminders to friends and colleagues.

RescueTime

If you have doubts if you are using your time sensibly, this app will send you weekly reports to indicate what things are stealing your time. Aside from its automatic time-tracking feature, this app also has distraction blocking and efficient report tools. RescueTime helps you avoid being overwhelmed by too many tasks. This app also has team software that’s dedicated to time management among team members. With this app, you may be shocked to discover how much time you’re wasting.

Clockify

Clockify is a time tracking software used by over two million people. It’s a popular time tracker and timesheet app that lets you track work hours across projects. Clockify is available for an unlimited number of users, completely free. It works in your browser, but you can also install it on your phone or computer for convenience.

LastPass

LastPass gives you a secure way to create, manage, and store your passwords so you never have to hit “Forgot password?” again. With the LastPass browser extension, a user can automatically fill in passwords when they are saved to their vault.

Trello

Trello is a flexible project management web application that lets users easily sort through their tasks and collaborate with their teammates. Instead of getting lost in a mess of emails or a confusing spreadsheet, Trello visualizes projects as lists pinned to digital bulletin boards. Each list is made of individual posts called cards, which can represent anything from tasks to ideas to goals. Team members can add comments, attachments, checklists, and custom labels to cards, keeping everyone on the same page.

Technical requirements

For Spread to function, the system it is running on must have the ability to receive inputs from a mouse or touch screen and receive a good amount of text so people can enter in tasks and information. The app will function best with an Internet connection, most likely with WiFi on mobile devices, alongside cloud syncing abilities, allowing it to sync between devices whenever possible. The system should load pages in under 3 seconds and store them locally in case the cloud connection is lost. The system should be able to allow the app access to other pieces of information on the device, such as related emails and messages. The user should be able to disable things like cloud service and disk access without degradation in any other app functionality.

Scenarios

Scenario 1

Martha is a student and learns that she has homework due before her class on Thursday. Before she forgets, she opens the Spread app on her phone or laptop and enters in the assignment name under her self-made “School” tasks. By typing a quick tag, she can set a reminder for Wednesday evening that will be automatically added to her calendar and will send her a notification when the time comes. She can also add any notes, links, or other information to follow with the entry. She completes the assignment the next day, checks it off on her task list, and satisfyingly watches it go into her done folder while checking off on her calendar.

Scenario 2

James is at an auto shop and has been told that he needs to make another appointment for getting new tires installed alongside getting a new recall taken care of. The mechanic asks him if he wants to make another appointment then. James takes out his phone and opens Spread, finds that he is free on Friday, and asks to schedule the appointment then. He then adds a new calendar appointment to his Spread Calendar and also inputs a reminder, information on the tires he needs to choose, and the recall number. The appointment and notifications then automatically sync between his devices and creates a reminder on his task list to prepare for the appointment.

Scenario 3

Sid has just been employed at a new company where they have received an email saying they need to complete quarterly training to stay current. The Spread app comes up with a suggestion to add these trainings as tasks. They need to complete them now, but they are also reoccurring. Sid opens the app and adds tags to the reminders which will make them repeat once per quarter, adding calendar events, notifications, and new tasks each time they need to be done. Sid also adds links and usernames for the trainings so it is easier to find them later. Now, every time Sid needs to renew their certifications, Spread will remind them and have each one on their calendar and task list.

Use cases

Person 1 using the Spread iOS app can see a calendar view of all tasks, events, and information attached to a time by opening the menu and pressing the calendar link.

Person 2 using the Spread Android app can see a calendar view of all tasks, events, and information attached to a time by opening the menu and pressing the calendar link.

Person 3 using the Spread desktop app can see a calendar view of all tasks, events, and information attached to a time by typing a shortcut.

Person 1 using the Spread iOS app can add a reminder to a task by pressing the clock icon next to a task and selecting a reminder option.

Person 2 using the Spread Android app can add a reminder to a task by pressing the clock icon next to a task and selecting a reminder option.

Person 3 using the Spread desktop app can add a reminder to a task by typing in a tag which turns into a reminder for that task.

Person 1 using the Spread iOS app can add a series of information to remember to the app by selecting email or message text and pressing the option to import into Spread as a note.

Person 2 using the Spread Android app can add a series of information to remember to the app by selecting email or message text and pressing the option to import into Spread as a note.

Person 3 using the Spread desktop app can add a series of information to remember to the app by right-clicking the email or message text and selecting the option to import into Spread as a note.
